Legal FAQs

Common questions answered by Learn With Mr. Oddlaw — because knowing the rules is how you win.

1. What does “pro se” mean?

“Pro se” is Latin for “on one’s own behalf.” It means you are representing yourself in court without hiring a lawyer. Thousands of Americans do it every year — but success depends on understanding procedure, evidence, and court rules. That’s where Mr. Oddlaw’s lessons begin.

2. Can I really win in court without a lawyer?

Yes — if you learn the rules. The courtroom runs on procedure, not emotion. By mastering how to allege the facts, prove the facts, and move the judge, you can present your case with the same tactical precision lawyers use.

3. What is the “3-Step Formula” I keep hearing about?

It’s the backbone of courtroom strategy:

  • Step 1 – Allege the Facts: Tell the court exactly what happened and what rule was broken.
  • Step 2 – Prove the Facts: Support every statement with admissible evidence — documents, testimony, or exhibits.
  • Step 3 – Move the Judge: File written motions and requests that compel legal action in your favor.

This formula, taught in the How to Win in Court Course, has helped everyday people stand toe-to-toe with attorneys.
