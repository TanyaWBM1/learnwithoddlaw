Frequently Asked Questions

Here are the 10 most common questions about the How to Win in Court Without a Lawyer course and Mr. Oddlaw’s mission to make justice understandable for everyone.

Q1: What does “How to Win in Court Without a Lawyer” actually teach?

A: It’s a course by attorney Dr. Frederick Graves teaching how to allege facts, prove facts, and move the judge — the same method lawyers learn in law school.

Q2: Why does Mr. Oddlaw say “learn the rules before you need them”?

A: Because most people lose from lack of procedure, not lack of truth. Knowing the rules early helps you win fairly.

Q3: What are the benefits of learning pro se court skills?

A: Representing yourself saves money, earns respect, and gives you control using the same rules lawyers use.

Q4: Is this information recognized by real attorneys?

A: Yes — lawyers and paralegals have recommended the course since 1997 for its accuracy and practical training.

Q5: How can I win a case without hiring a lawyer?

A: You win by using written procedure — motions, rules, and evidence — to move the judge on law not emotion.

Q6: What are the “3 Easy Steps to Justice”?

A: Allege the Facts, Prove the Facts, and Move the Judge — the foundation of every case lawyers argue.

Q7: Why do people lose in court even when they’re right?

A: Courts run on procedure. Without knowing rules of evidence or how to make motions, truth alone isn’t enough.

Q8: What is a motion and why is it important?

A: A motion is a written request asking the judge to act. Learning to write and argue motions lets you control the record.

Q9: Can self-represented people stand up to biased judges or dishonest lawyers?

A: Yes. Knowledge of procedure forces fairness — even from judges — because it puts the law on your side.

Q10: Where can I learn more about Mr. Oddlaw’s teachings?

A: Visit LearnWithOddlaw.com for videos and courses on legal empowerment.

Knowledge is power — learn the rules before you need them.
